# Referee Hand Signals for 3-Point Shots (NFHS / High School Basketball)

## 3-Point Attempt
- **Signal:** One hand raised vertically above the head.
- **Which hand:** Typically the lead hand (the hand closer to the basket direction), but either hand may be used.
- **Meaning:** Indicates that the shot attempt is a three-point field goal attempt.

## Made 3-Point Shot
- **Signal:** Both hands raised vertically above the head.
- **Meaning:** Indicates that the three-point shot attempt was successful (good).

## Additional Context
- These signals are made by the lead official (or any official) after a shot attempt is released.
- The signal is held briefly to communicate to the scorer's table, players, and fans.
- Other common referee signals (for reference):
  - **Timeout:** Both hands forming a "T".
  - **Foul:** One hand clinched into a fist, pulled upward from the waist.
  - **Held ball:** Two fists thrust upward simultaneously.
  - **Substitution signal:** Hands rotating around each other.
  - **Shot clock violation:** Hand tapped on the head.
  - **Three-second violation:** Three fingers extended, arm moved side to side.

*Note: This summary is based on publicly available NFHS mechanics and typical officiating guides.*
